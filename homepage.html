Who Am I?

I'm a Girl Who Codes!

Why did I create this website?

I just spent a whole summer teaching, learning, and getting inspired by twenty female high school students who joined me in my mission to close gender gap in technology. We did this by being part of a summer immersion program hosted by Girls Who Code and sponsored by Autodesk San Francisco. A summer of teaching girls in a city that is probably the Mecca of Tech inspired me to use my tech skills to create a website about Math! Which is something I am going to school for right now. Yes, I am still in school and I taught for Girls Who Code for the second time over the summer. I am currently pursuing a Master's in applied Math at University of Missouri-Columbia. I also teach college algebra and finite mathematics as a teaching assistant. This website is a collection of some of my personal projects I worked on this summer that combine my finite math information with things I taught and learned while teaching this summer.

Want to know more about Girls Who Code, Mizzou, or Me?


Girls Who Code is a non-profit organization that works towards closing the gender gap in technology. Their mission is to encourage women to pursue STEM careers by exposing them to basic programming languages while they are in still in high school. Check them out!

The University of Missouri is a public land-grant research university located in Columbia, Missouri. As the largest university in the state, it is the flagship campus of the University of Missouri System, which also maintains campuses in Kansas City, Rolla, and St. Louis. MU is one of only 60 public and private U.S. universities invited to membership in the prestigious Association of American Universities. AAU membership helps Mizzou attract external funding and recruit top faculty and students.
